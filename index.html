About Us

We make it easier for students admitted in world's top 200 universities to fund their education by pledging their most valuable asset - intellectual capital. Today, especially as more students take out private loans with high interest rates, many graduates struggle to make their monthly payments. Some of these graduates default, causing long-term credit problems for themselves, and costing lenders money. Others shape their career choices around the need to pay back their loans. Life could be easier, and better. The solution is simple. Students give a share of their income to investors for a number of years. Since payments are scaled to wages, the odds of default - and of financial hardship for the graduate - are greatly reduced.

Investors

If the students earn handsomely, the investors stand to gain more than they would under a traditional loan. Upvego would essentially be creating a new type of asset. Based on the wages of college graduates it would offer investors another way to diversify their portfolios. An educational fund offers you as an investor a very steady flow. There is a protection against inflation. If inflation goes up, the income will go up. Or you could be motivated by philanthropic goals. Wealthy alumni might see this as a way to help students attend their high-priced alma maters.

Foundations and Institutions

Contracts are more sustainable alternative to scholarships. Instead of doling out money with no strings attached, as in a conventional scholarship, foundations could require students to sign contracts. Same goes out for institutions as well. These would state that nothing is owed up to a certain point, but high-earning graduates would repay a percentage of their income, allowing the foundation to recycle that money into later classes.

Universities and Colleges

Universities and colleges can invest in their students via income share agreements or Pay It Forward (financial aid policy). It's a win-win situation for both students and the institutions. Through ISA investors' profit is closely tied to the student's success. They offer students better terms for programs that are expected to be of high value and have strong incentives to support students both during school and after graduation. This process gives students strong signals about which programs and fields are most likely to help them be successful. It would also help stem tuition inflation and improve the efficiency of the higher education system by rewarding high-quality, low-cost programs.

Questions & answers

What do you mean by shares of future income?
We want to help students in their career by making it easier to afford expensive college fees. A student can pledge about share of their income, which is calculated on the basis of their future income and the investment by the investor, for 7-10 years. The time a student spends in unemployment won't be considered. This way, it won't be a burden on them. Also, in case the student doesn't end up graduating and drops out, he has to bear the cost of the losses.
What makes me eligible as a student to apply?
All that we need is a proof of your admission in world's top 200 colleges and universities. Priority will be given to students who can't take loans, have a major with high employment rates or show high intellectual potential.
Will I get funds directly in my bank account?
No. We'll be collaborating with your colleges and be your official sponsor. Your fees will be sent to the college directly from our offices.
I have met all the eligibility requirements. Am I sure to get funding?
Upvego isn't a panacea for all the problems faced by a student in financing. We consider your holistic profile before deciding to fund you. Your academic history, results from psychometric tests, your essays, college's reputation, major you're admitted into, interview results, and several other aspects of your profile play an important role in whether you receive funding or not. We'll try our best, but the final call is taken by investors.
